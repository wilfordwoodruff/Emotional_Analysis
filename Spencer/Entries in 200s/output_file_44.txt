 During the month of January, 1880 I was at Sunset, Arizona, with Bro. Lot Smith and the brethrenwith him who were trying to es-tablish a branch of the United Order at that place. At this time the Government, through its of-ficers, was using every means inits power to enforce the Edmunds-Tucker anti-polygamy law withthe evident intent on the part of the officers, to break us up as an organized community.Being away from PresidentTaylor and my Quorum, I felt deeply distressed in mind concerning our condition as apeople. While thus exercised I went into "the Wilderness,"a region of country called by this name situated about forty miles west of Sunset;and while there I stopped with two young men who wereherding sheep belonging tothe people of Sunset.
